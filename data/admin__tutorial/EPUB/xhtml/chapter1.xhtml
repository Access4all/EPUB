1.1. Headings and sections

The most important when you write a long text is to split it into chapter and sections; each section must have an heading introducing it, the text headings and sections above for the current section. That heading is of level 2 because we are still Under chapter 1. Level 1 is the greatest level and the smallest possible level is 6. For common texts, there is rare needs for levels 5 and 6, even books with 500 pages.

Structuring your text into headings is very important :

  • Screen reader users use heading structure as navigation, to jump and command them to start reading where headings are. Without any headings, screen readers have no choice but going a bit forward and backward till they find the beginning of the section, a little like when you had to find the beginning of a song on a audio K7.
  • As we will see in the next chapter, you can make a link precisely referencing a section or a subsection

Usually, we recommand that each file has a single heading level 1. It is also a good practice to separate your book in several files, for an easier eidition but also for better reading. You can try to create a new file by pressing the correspondig button on the top bar.

Expert trick: you can check your heading hierarchy by refreshing table of contents.
